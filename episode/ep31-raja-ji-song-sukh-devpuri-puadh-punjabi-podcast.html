📖 Sukh Devpuri's Spiritual Journey

From short hair to dastar — Waheguru's hand in every step.

2013
Embraced Sikhi Saroop

Sukhchain began his journey on the Sikh path. Before this, he had short hair and less beard — a different chapter of life.

2018
Formed His Jatha

A friend called him knowing his powerful voice. They formed a Jatha together and began performing at Nagar Keertans and Darbars.

2022
Raja Sahib Shabad Released in July

The viral shabad "Asi Aaye Haan Tere Duar Raja Ji" was released. It crossed 10 million views on YouTube alone, with millions more on reels and Facebook.

Late 2022
A Quiet Step Back

Fame brought its own tests. Sukh stepped away from the limelight, changed his number, and moved into quiet domestic life — "sarkal chhota kar liya."

2026
Maharaj's Hukam — He's Back!

By Waheguru's grace, Sardar Bahadur Singh of Kakron honoured him with the new shabad "Raja Ji Da Dar" — and Sukh Devpuri is back stronger than ever, with a dastar and full beard.

💬 Big Topics Discussed in This Episode

Phones, Youth & Punjab's Future

Children given phones young cannot grow mentally. Sukh Devpuri passionately supports banning phones in schools and teaching Gatka — Sikhs' own martial art — to youth instead of gym selfies.

Gatka — The Forgotten Martial Art

Sikh warriors were fearless because they practiced Gatka and lived in the wild. Today's youth spending on gym powders and selfies should learn Gatka instead — especially for women's safety.

Punjab's History & Divided Land

From Maharaja Ranjit Singh's Punjab (1.4 lakh sq miles, stretching to Tibet & Rajasthan) to today's divided state — deep conversation on how Himachal and Haryana were cut away in 1966.

Keeping Your Inner Circle Small

"Jado teri charhai si, dost bahut si. Jado pichhe hatiyan, koi nahi." — Sukh's hard-won wisdom: trust few, count on fingers, and never trust those who show one face in front and another behind.

Deep Singh Sidhu & Punjab Voices

A thoughtful conversation on how Sidhu Moosewala, Deep Sidhu and others — who seemed far from Punjab's issues — suddenly stood up for the land. Waheguru uses unexpected instruments.

Punjab's Shrinking Green Cover

Trees are being cut for roads, kheti is gone, buildings 70 feet deep break the water table. "Pehlan ik barf wali thandi hundi si, huni itni garmi kyun?" — connecting deforestation to climate change the desi way.

🙏 Sukh Devpuri's Message for Everyone

"ਸਰੀਰ ਤੋਂ ਹੋ ਜਾਂਗੇ ਬੇਮੁੱਖ — ਮਨ ਤੋਂ ਨਹੀਂ ਹੋ ਸਕਦੇ।"

You may drift outwardly, but what Maharaj has placed in your soul cannot be taken away.

🎙️ Sukh's Advice for Aspiring Singers

YouTube is Your Ustad Now

Search any raag, any style — it's all there. Today's young singers learn faster than ever because of technology. Use it wisely.

But a Real Ustad is Still Irreplaceable

"Ustad di bhanda hundi hai ek kiSam di." A real teacher gives you something algorithms cannot — heart, timing, and the subtleties of raag that only come through human transmission.

Erase Negativity. Think "I Can."

"Negativity kabhi nahi likhya mere ton — yeh kaam nahi hona." Never say "I can't." Even if you fail the first time, try again. Only those who try can fail — and only those who fail can learn to succeed.

Stay Connected to Your Public — Always

"Sangat ch pangat." If someone calls or messages, reply. Your audience's love is your real strength — not industry connections or stars. The moment you become arrogant, you lose everything.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Who is Sukh Devpuri?
Sukh Devpuri (real name Sukhchain Singh Devpuri) is a Punjabi devotional singer from the Puadh region. He is best known for his viral Raja Sahib shabad that crossed 10 million views. He began his Sikhi path in 2013, formed his Jatha in 2018, and has released 25+ devotional tracks. He was previously featured on Puadh Punjabi Podcast and returns in Episode 31.
What is the new Raja Ji Da Dar song about?
The new shabad "Raja Ji Da Dar" is written by Sardar Bahadur Singh of Kakron. It is a heartfelt devotional plea at the door of Raja Ji (Baba Raja Sahib Ji), expressing that the devotee has found no door as beautiful in the whole world. The song beautifully captures surrender, longing, and gratitude.
How many views did Sukh Devpuri's original Raja Sahib shabad get?
The original Raja Sahib shabad crossed 10 million (1 crore) views on YouTube, with millions more from Instagram reels and Facebook. Many people also set it as their phone ringtone, and it is still played at langars and diwans across Punjab and abroad.
Why did Sukh Devpuri stop making music for a while?
After the success of his first shabad, Sukh Devpuri stepped back from active music. He changed his number, kept his circle very small, and went back to a quiet domestic life. He describes it as "mind thoda corrupt ho gaya" — the pressures of fame and people around him affected his peace. He is now back by Maharaj's grace.
Where can I listen to the full Episode 31 interview?
