Why Not Just Record at Home?

Fair question. Here's the honest answer: you can. And for audio-only podcasts on a tight budget, a decent USB mic and a quiet room works fine.

But if you're doing a video podcast — which is what gets traction on YouTube and Instagram in 2026 — the gap between home recording and studio recording is enormous. Here's what home recording typically produces:

  • Echo and room noise that no software can fully remove
  • Flat, unflattering camera angles (laptop webcam or phone propped on books)
  • Bad lighting that makes everyone look tired and unprofessional
  • Hours of editing time that you probably don't have
  • No 6 Reels — meaning your episode disappears instead of getting discovered

The reason 90% of podcasts fail isn't the content — it's the production. Viewers judge quality in the first 5 seconds. A professional setup signals credibility before you've said a single word.

The Real Cost of Home Recording

A decent home setup (camera, mic, lighting, acoustic panels, editing software) costs ₹80,000–₹1,50,000 upfront — and still won't match a professional studio. For the cost of 7–10 studio sessions, you can produce 7–10 episodes, build an audience, and decide if podcasting is right for you before spending that kind of money.
